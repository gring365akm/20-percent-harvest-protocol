Part 1 · My SOFI Options Engine

How I Turn One SOFI LEAP Into an Income Stream

This is the 4-step structure I use: buy a long-term SOFI LEAP, sell covered calls against it, grab 20% profit, and repeat. The LEAP stays long-term. The calls provide the cash flow.

1
Buy the SOFI LEAP
  • I start by buying a long-dated SOFI call option (my LEAP).
  • This behaves like synthetic share ownership with a much lower upfront cost than 100 shares.
  • It becomes the foundation I use to sell covered calls.
Example: SOFI $2 Call · Jan 2026
2
Sell a Covered Call
  • Because the LEAP behaves like shares, I can sell a short-term covered call on SOFI.
  • This brings in premium up front and turns the LEAP into an income-producing asset.
Example: Sell SOFI $8.50 Call (near-term)
3
Close Around 20% Profit
  • When the covered call reaches about 20% profit, I buy it back and close the position.
  • I don’t wait until expiration. I choose a fast, controlled win over hoping for bigger but slower gains.
Rule: Hit ~20% → Harvest the win
4
Repeat the Cycle
  • After closing the call, I immediately sell another covered call on the same LEAP.
  • Each cycle adds more premium while the LEAP stays in place as a long-term position.
Leap stays · Calls rotate
Part 2 · Why 20% Accelerates Growth

How Quick 20% Wins Boost My Income Velocity

Taking profit at 20% and repeating the covered call cycle gives me faster rotation, more trades, and smoother equity curves than simply holding calls to expiration.

A
Faster Rotation
  • 20% targets often hit within a few days instead of 30+ days.
  • My LEAP is not trapped in one trade; it’s free to earn multiple times per month.
More cycles = more premium
B
Compounding Premium
  • Every “sell → 20% → close → resell” cycle stacks one small win on top of the last.
  • Instead of one slow gain, I compound multiple smaller profits on the same LEAP.
Stack small wins, not big wishes
C
Lower Risk, Higher Consistency
  • Closing early avoids the wild “gamma” swings near expiration.
  • I lock in gains before sharp reversals or surprise moves can erase them.
  • Fewer big drawdowns = a smoother equity curve and higher confidence.
Protect the account first
D
My LEAP as a Rental Property
  • I treat the SOFI LEAP like a rental house.
  • The covered call premium is my “rent.”
  • By closing at 20% and re-renting quickly, I maximize cash flow from a single long-term asset.
One LEAP · Many “rent checks”
